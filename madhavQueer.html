Pride Flags & Their Significance

Pride Flag

The rainbow flag represents the diversity and unity of the LGBTQ+ community. Each color symbolizes different aspects: life, healing, sunlight, nature, harmony, and spirit.

Trans Pride Flag

The transgender pride flag features blue, pink, and white stripes, representing traditional gender colors and non-binary identities, celebrating gender diversity and transition.

Bisexual Pride Flag

The bisexual flag uses pink, purple, and blue to represent attraction to multiple genders, with purple symbolizing the blending of gender attractions.

Non-binary Pride Flag

The non-binary flag includes yellow, white, purple, and black stripes, representing gender outside the binary, gender multiplicity, and those who feel without gender.

Genderqueer Pride Flag

The genderqueer flag uses lavender, white, and green to represent non-binary gender identities, androgyny, and gender expressions outside traditional norms.

Asexual Pride Flag

The asexual flag features black, grey, white, and purple stripes, representing the asexual spectrum, including romantic relationships and community.

At Madav Queer Restro, we proudly display and honor these flags as symbols of identity, unity, and respect for all members of our diverse community.
